Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple and widely used tool to assess an individual's weight relative to their height. It helps identify potential health risks associated with being underweight, overweight, or obese. By calculating BMI, healthcare professionals can monitor trends in weight-related health conditions, provide guidelines for maintaining a healthy weight, and promote awareness of balanced lifestyle choices. Although BMI does not account for muscle mass or fat distribution, it serves as a quick screening method to highlight potential health concerns.
